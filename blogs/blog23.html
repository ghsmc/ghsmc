IKEA

The date is Tuesday, July 6. The time is 11:13:56 PM. I'm in Long Island, New York.

Today's blog is about what I've been doing all day. My neck hurts; my back aches. I've been building IKEA furniture.

For the longest time, I've needed a proper place to work in my room. I've had janky setups around the house — computer hanging off the side of the too-small table, cords everywhere, and spotty wifi. I went out to IKEA and picked up a desk chair and a standing desk. It's huge. I bought it in two seperate pieces — a huge wooden kitchen island, and a electronic "up-and-down" underframe.

It's great. It has an electronic lever that allows me to control the desk's height. I can work while sitting down, or I can work while standing up. It's sort of stupid, and it is stupidly expensive, but it's a really nice desk. To show you the progression of the desk's construction, here's an image gallery.
