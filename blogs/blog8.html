An Inconclusive End

The time is 10:11:55 PM. The date is Tuesday, June 8. Feeling great.

To the future me reading this, the time between this blog and the previous blog has been an absolute grind. I've just finished ten page paper on the IMF in Zambia. I turned in an essay on Emily Dickinson's "They Shut me up in Prose." I took a sit-down, 2-hour, cumulative math test. I took a Spanish exam this morning. It's been fun, honestly. My work doesn't feel rigirous when I do it around my friends, and the freedom of finals is generous. Otherwise, I'm preparing for the summer: organizing my goals, my projects, and my things.

But now, I'm done. It's over. It's a surreal feeling, really. Everything I've done in the last year is wrapped up nicely; Nothing left for me to do — all tests taken, essays turned in, homework completed. I'm underwhelmed. Maybe I was expecting an instant liberation, a blissful escape from the grind. But I haven't had that yet. The moment one problem dissapears, another consumes the mind.

I thought the last post left things inconclusively. This one clarifies it. Feeling great, though I haven't really processed what it means that I have nothing left. I'm excited to see how everythinng goes this summer.

I love the clarity these posts give my mind, even if I'm just talking to myself. Expect more posts this summer, but don't expect consistency. I won't demand that of myself. I think any deadlines, constraints on my expression, or requirements for cosnistent posting contradict the purpose of this blog altogether. No need for that here — I get my good fix at Lawrenceville.

Signing off on Blog 8. It's 10:20:56 PM.
